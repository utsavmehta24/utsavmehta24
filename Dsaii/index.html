Hugging Face launches open source AI assistant maker to rival OpenAI’s custom GPTs
Hugging Face has launched Hugging Chat Assistants, a free, customizable AI assistant maker that rivals OpenAI's subscription-based custom GPTs.
The new tool allows users to choose from a variety of open source large language models (LLMs) for their AI assistants, unlike OpenAI's reliance on proprietary models.
An aggregator page for third-party customized Hugging Chat Assistants mimics OpenAI's GPT Store, offering users various assistants to choose from and use.

Bridging the Gap: From education to Industry

This column explores the transition from your academic journey in data science and AI to a fulfilling career in the industry. Here, we'll tackle the challenges and opportunities you might encounter, providing insights and resources to help you bridge the gap Smoothly.


  • Data Analyst: Gather, clean, and prepare data for analysis, generating reports and visualizations to inform decision-making.
  • Data Scientist: Analyze and interpret data to extract insights, build predictive models, and solve complex business problems.
  • Machine Learning Engineer: Design, develop, and implement machine learning algorithms to automate tasks and create intelligent systems
  • Business Intelligence Analyst: Translate data insights into actionable recommendations for businesses, supporting strategic planning and growth.

To thrive in this dynamic field, it's crucial to stay updated on the latest trends and technologies. Here are some tips:
  • Continuously learn and upskill: Take online courses, attend workshops, and participate in hackathons to stay relevant.
  • Build a strong portfolio: Showcase your skills and experience through personal projects, internships, and research contributions.
  • Network actively: Connect with professionals in the field, attend industry events, and build your professional network.
  • Develop soft skills: Communication, teamwork, critical thinking, and problem-solving are essential for success in any industry.
